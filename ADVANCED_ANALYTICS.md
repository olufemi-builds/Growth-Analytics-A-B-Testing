# Advanced Analytics

## Time-Series Forecasting

Time-series forecasting involves predicting future values based on previously observed values. It is essential for businesses to make decisions based on trends identified over time. Common methods include:
- **ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average)**: A popular method for understanding and predicting future points in a series.
- **Exponential Smoothing**: A technique that applies decreasing weights to past observations.

## Cohort Analysis and Retention Metrics

Cohort analysis is the examination of a group's behavior within a particular time frame. Retention metrics measure the percentage of users who continue to engage with a product over specified periods, giving insights into customer loyalty and product performance. 

### Analyzing Retention Metrics:
1. Define your cohorts based on user characteristics or acquisition date.
2. Calculate retention rates over various periods (daily, weekly, monthly).

## Multi-Variant Testing Approaches

Multi-variant testing involves testing multiple variables at the same time to determine which combination performs best. This method goes beyond traditional A/B testing, enabling a more comprehensive understanding of user interactions.

### Differences from A/B Testing:
- A/B Testing: Tests two variations against each other.
- Multi-Variant Testing: Tests multiple variables simultaneously, allowing for more complex insights.

## Causal Inference Techniques

Causal inference seeks to identify and establish cause-and-effect relationships between variables. It is crucial for determining the impact of interventions.

### Common Methods Include:
- **Regression Analysis**: Understanding relationships between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables.
- **Randomized Controlled Trials (RCT)**: A method where participants are randomly assigned to different groups for testing.

These topics are areas of interest for advanced analytics, providing frameworks and methodologies for data-driven decision-making in businesses.
