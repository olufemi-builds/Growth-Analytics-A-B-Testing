# Project Case Studies

## Overview
This document provides a collection of end-to-end case studies that illustrate the business context, data pipeline processes, statistical methodologies, results including confidence intervals, and ROI analysis associated with various A/B testing initiatives.

---

## Case Study 1: Impact of Website Redesign on Conversion Rates

### Business Context
In 2025, company X sought to improve their online sales through a complete redesign of their website. Previous data indicated a high bounce rate and low conversion rates.

### Data Pipeline
1. **Data Collection**: User activity was tracked using Google Analytics and custom events.
2. **Data Cleaning**: Data was cleaned using Python scripts to handle missing values and outliers.
3. **Data Storage**: Processed data was stored in a relational database for easy access and querying.

### Statistical Methodology
An A/B test was conducted where Group A experienced the old design and Group B the new design. A significance level of 0.05 was used for hypothesis testing.

### Results
- **Conversion Rate (Old Design)**: 2.5% 
- **Conversion Rate (New Design)**: 4.0% 
- **Confidence Interval**: [1.1%, 2.1%]  
This result indicates a statistically significant increase in conversion rates.

### ROI Analysis
The projected increase in revenue was estimated based on the expected increase in sales from the higher conversion rate, leading to an estimated ROI of 150%.

## Case Study 2: Email Marketing Campaign Effectiveness

### Business Context
In 2025, company X launched an email marketing campaign targeting users who had not purchased in the last 6 months.

### Data Pipeline
1. **Data Collection**: Email engagement metrics were collected using MailChimp.
2. **Data Cleaning**: Non-responsive users were filtered out to avoid skewing the results.
3. **Data Storage**: Engagement data was stored in a cloud database for analysis.

### Statistical Methodology
A controlled experiment compared two types of email content (promotional vs. informational). Analysis used a Z-test for proportions.

### Results
- **Response Rate (Promotional)**: 10% 
- **Response Rate (Informational)**: 5% 
- **Confidence Interval**: [4%, 8%]  
The promotional email led to a significantly higher response rate.

### ROI Analysis
The increased sales attributed to the campaign resulted in an estimated ROI of 200%.
